**Project Title: Geographic Patterns in Ticket Sales: 2023–2025 CHaD Battle of the Badges**

**Project Overview**

This project analyzes multi-year ticket sales and fundraising performance  using real-world data from the Children’s Hospital at Dartmouth Health (CHaD).

The Battle of the Badges is a premier fundraising event where local "badged" professionals (police, 

fire, and rescue) compete in a hockey match to raise funds for the hospital's pediatric programs. It 

relies on community engagement and ticket sales to support life-saving care for children.

The objective of this analysis is to address the following business question: What geographical gaps exist within the ticket sales data? 

This project is a real-world analytics consulting engagement, where the goal is to translate data into actionable insights for decision-makers at CHaD.

**Analytical Approach**

The analysis applies descriptive analytics techniques to identify patterns and trends, including:

Time-based analysis: Year-over-year comparisons from 2023 to 2025. 

Geographic analysis: Identification of Manchester as the primary stronghold and tracking expansion into northern Massachusetts. 

Comparative analysis: Evaluating the effectiveness of new players (28% of the roster) in reaching new communities compared to returning participants. 

Data visualization using Tableau: Utilizing heatmaps and geographic distribution charts to visualize market density. 

**Key Insights**

Insight 1: Manchester remains the primary stronghold, consistently producing the highest volume of player participation and ticket sales. 

Insight 2: Reach is successfully expanding into southern New Hampshire and northern Massachusetts, specifically towns like Danvers, Malden, and Andover. 

Insight 3: There is a significant reliance on new talent; 28% of the 2025 roster are new players, and they are identified as the primary drivers for expanding CHaD’s reach into untapped communities. 

**Business Recommendations**

Recommendation 1: Invest in professional electronic streaming to eliminate the "geographic ceiling" caused by physical attendance barriers, allowing global supporters to contribute. 

Recommendation 2: Expand targeted marketing efforts beyond New Hampshire into the broader New England region to capitalize on the networks of new players.

Recommendation 3: Develop a long-term digital strategy to transition CHaD from a regional fundraising model to a national model through digital engagement.
